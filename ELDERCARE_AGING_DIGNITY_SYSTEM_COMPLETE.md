## The Replacement System

### Core Model: Community-Based Aging in Place

#### Structure
```
HOME-BASED CARE (Primary)
├── Personal care assistants (bathing, dressing, meals)
├── Healthcare visits (nurses, therapists, doctors)
├── Home modifications (accessibility, safety)
├── Meal delivery and nutrition support
└── Technology support (telehealth, monitoring)

COMMUNITY HUBS (Neighborhood)
├── Daily activity centers (social, meals, programs)
├── Wisdom councils (elder governance, mentorship)
├── Intergenerational programs (childcare co-location)
├── Healthcare clinics
└── Respite care for family caregivers

COOPERATIVE HOUSING (Option)
├── Small-scale co-housing (6-12 elders)
├── Shared care staff, private spaces
├── Community meals and activities
├── Peer support and friendship
└── Aging in community, not institution

SPECIALIZED SUPPORT (As Needed)
├── Memory care (dementia-friendly environments)
├── Hospice and palliative care
├── Skilled nursing (short-term rehabilitation)
└── Crisis intervention
```

#### Operating Principles
1. **Home first**: Default is aging at home with supports
2. **Community integration**: Elders remain part of neighborhoods
3. **Autonomy**: Elders control their own lives and decisions
4. **Intergenerational**: Connection across ages, not segregation
5. **Holistic**: Address physical, social, emotional, spiritual needs
6. **Culturally responsive**: Respect diverse aging practices

## Specific Solutions

### Tier 1: Universal Home-Based Care
**Who**: Every person 65+ (or younger if disabled)
**What**:
- Personal care assistance (as needed, 2-40 hours/week)
- Healthcare coordination and home visits
- Home modifications for accessibility/safety
- Meal delivery (1-3 meals/day as needed)
- Transportation to appointments and activities
- Technology setup and support
- Zero cost to elders or families

**Quality Standards**:
- Worker ratio: 1:4 maximum for personal care
- Staff: Living wage ($22+/hour), full benefits, training
- Consistency: Same caregivers (relationship building)
- Autonomy: Elder directs care, chooses workers when possible

### Tier 2: Community Hub Services
**Who**: All elders, voluntary participation
**What**:
- Daily drop-in centers (meals, activities, socialization)
- Wisdom councils (elder governance and mentorship)
- Intergenerational programs (co-located with childcare)
- Healthcare screening and services
- Education and enrichment programs
- Volunteer coordination (elders as volunteers)

**Access**: Walking distance or free transportation

### Tier 3: Cooperative Housing Option
**Who**: Elders who choose community living
**What**:
- Small group homes (6-12 people)
- Private bedrooms, shared common spaces
- 24/7 care staff on-site
- Community meals, activities, support
- Costs covered by universal system

**Model**: Similar to Green House Project, not institutional nursing homes

### Tier 4: Specialized Care
**Who**: Elders with dementia, complex medical needs, end-of-life
**What**:
- Memory care (home-based or small group homes)
- Skilled nursing (short-term rehab, not long-term warehousing)
- Hospice and palliative care (home-based priority)
- Crisis intervention and stabilization

**Approach**: Small-scale, person-centered, time-limited when possible

## Worker Justice Core

### Fair Compensation
- Starting wage: $22/hour minimum (home care aides)
- Experience ladder: Up to $40/hour (specialized care, long tenure)
- Full benefits: Healthcare, retirement, paid leave
- Hazard pay for difficult conditions
- Paid training and professional development

### Working Conditions
- Manageable caseloads (not maximized)
- Travel time paid
- Consistent schedules (not on-call chaos)
- Safety protections (lifting equipment, safety protocols)
- Mental health support for workers

### Career Pathways
- Entry: No degree required, paid training
- Advancement: Nurse aide → LPN → RN support
- Specializations: Dementia care, hospice, rehabilitation
- Leadership: Care coordination, program management
- Cooperative ownership: Worker-owned care agencies

- Union representation

### Self-Determination
Elders have the right to:
- Choose their own care workers
- Refuse treatments or interventions
- Control their own schedules and routines
- Manage their own finances
- Make their own decisions (unless legally incapacitated)

### Guardianship Reform
- Supported decision-making (not full guardianship default)
- Elder representation in all guardianship proceedings
- Regular review and least restrictive approach
- Abuse prevention and monitoring
- Elder voice prioritized

### End-of-Life Rights
- Advance directives honored
- Hospice and palliative care access
- Death with dignity options
- Natural death support
- Spiritual/cultural practices respected
