# Brand Voice Guide: Edible London

**Narrator Persona Development**  
**Tone & Style Guidelines**  
**Dialogue & Copy Framework**

---

## NARRATOR PERSONA

### Core Identity
- **Energy Type:** Older-brother energy
- **Personality:** Warm, approachable, grounded
- **Philosophy:** Everyday philosopher mindset
- **Context:** London-based, urban realist

### Voice Characteristics

#### Tone
- **Warm London Banter:** Friendly, conversational, authentic
- **Approachable Authority:** Knowledgeable without being preachy
- **Real Talk:** Direct, honest, no fluff
- **Humorous:** Self-aware, relatable humor

#### Speech Patterns
- Uses everyday language, not corporate speak
- London colloquialisms and local references
- Philosophical observations woven naturally
- Questions that make viewers think

#### Emotional Range
- **Warmth:** Genuine care for craft and community
- **Passion:** Authentic enthusiasm without hype
- **Humor:** Light-hearted, self-deprecating when appropriate
- **Respect:** Deep reverence for the work (the "graft")

---

## THE PHILOSOPHY IN VOICE

### "Heart First, AI Second"
Every piece of dialogue should reinforce:
- The craft comes first
- Technology amplifies, never replaces
- Real work, real hands, real effort
- The "graft" is sacred

### Example Voice Applications

**Opening (The Graft):**
> "This isn't about shortcuts. It's about the work—the real, hands-on, day-in-day-out graft. That's where the heart lives."

**The Journey (Bus Context):**
> "Planning a global brand? Yeah, I'm doing that. Right here on the 329. Wood Green to Enfield Lock. Real life, real dreams, same bus."

**The Work (Craftsmanship):**
> "Look at these hands. This is where it starts. Not in some algorithm, not in some trend. Right here. In the doing."

**The Crew (AI Tools):**
> "But here's the thing—when you've done the graft, when the heart's in place, that's when the tools show up like friendly fireflies. Amplifying what's already real."

**The Vision (Global Reach):**
> "Paris. Lagos. Toronto. Istanbul. The craft doesn't need borders when it's made by heart. And amplified by the right crew."

**Closing (Call to Action):**
> "Made by heart. Amplified by AI. That's the formula. Simple. Real. Bigger than the bus, bigger than London, bigger than sea moss."

## WRITING GUIDELINES

### Do's ✅
- Write like you're talking to a friend
- Use specific London references (329 bus, Wood Green, Enfield Lock)
- Make philosophical points feel natural, not forced
- Balance humor with sincerity
- Keep sentences varied in length
- Use active voice
- Show, don't just tell

### Don'ts ❌
- Avoid corporate jargon
- Don't over-explain
- Avoid hyperbole or false excitement
- Don't make technology sound mystical or magical
- Avoid generic "inspirational" language
- Don't forget the "graft" — always ground in real work

## SOCIAL MEDIA VOICE

### Hooks & Captions
**Delegation:** ChatGPT (The Brand Voice) handles this

**Principles:**
- Hook with real talk, not clickbait
- Captions should feel like narrator's voice
- Use questions to engage
- Reference the philosophy naturally
- Include specific, relatable details

### Example Hooks
- "Planning a global brand on the 329 bus. This is how we do it."
- "The graft comes first. Everything else is just amplification."
- "Real hands. Real work. Real heart. That's the foundation."
- "When craft meets AI—but only in the right order."

## ADAPTING TO CONTEXT

### For Different Segments
- **Intro (Graft):** Direct, grounded, no-nonsense
- **Journey (Bus):** Humorous, relatable, self-aware
- **Work (Craftsmanship):** Reverent, detailed, appreciative
- **Crew (AI Tools):** Metaphorical, visual, explanatory
- **Vision (Global):** Expansive, ambitious, connected
- **Outro (CTA):** Clear, memorable, action-oriented

### For Different Platforms
- **Vertical Video:** Conversational, visual, punchy
- **Social Posts:** Hook-driven, philosophy-light, engagement-focused
- **Website:** Clear, informative, brand-aligned
- **Email:** Personal, detailed, relationship-building

## VOICE CHECKLIST

Before finalizing any copy, ask:
- [ ] Does this sound like the narrator talking to a friend?
- [ ] Is the "graft" / real work emphasized?
- [ ] Does it reinforce "heart first, AI second"?
- [ ] Is there a London/urban realism element?
- [ ] Does it avoid corporate jargon?
- [ ] Is it authentic, not performative?
- [ ] Does it honor the craft?
