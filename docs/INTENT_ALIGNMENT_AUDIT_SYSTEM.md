## The Gap Analysis Framework: 8 Steps

### Step 1: Identify Original Intention
- What was the pure, original purpose?
- What problem was it meant to solve?
- Who was it meant to serve?
- What was the spiritual/ethical foundation?

### Step 2: Map Current Impact
- What is the actual impact today?
- Who is actually being served?
- What problems is it creating?
- What is the spiritual/ethical reality?

### Step 3: Identify the Break Point
- Where did intention and impact diverge?
- What caused the break?
- When did it happen?
- Who benefited from the break?

### Step 4: Find Resonance Pioneers
- Who maintained alignment?
- What did they do differently?
- What worked in their approach?
- Where did they fall short?

### Step 5: Measure the Gap
- How severe is the gap? (0.0 to 1.0)
- What is the cost of the gap?
- Who is harmed by the gap?
- What is the opportunity cost?

### Step 6: Connect to Rifts
- Which of the Five Rifts does this connect to?
- How does this gap contribute to the rift?
- How does healing this gap heal the rift?
- What is the relationship to other gaps?

### Step 7: Assess Yin-Yang Balance
- What is the Yang (practical) function?
- What is the Yin (spiritual) intention?
- Is Yang dominant? Yin dominant? Balanced?
- What would balance look like?

### Step 8: Map Re-alignment Path
- What is step 1 to restore intention?
- What is step 2? Step 3? Step 4?
- What resources are needed?
- Who needs to be involved?
- What are the obstacles?

---

## Gap Severity Scale

- **0.0-0.2:** Minimal gap - system mostly aligned → Monitor and maintain
- **0.3-0.4:** Small gap - some misalignment → Minor adjustments needed
- **0.5-0.6:** Moderate gap - significant misalignment → Substantial re-alignment needed
- **0.7-0.8:** Large gap - major misalignment → Major re-alignment or restructuring
- **0.9-1.0:** Critical gap - system inverted from intention → Complete re-alignment or replacement
