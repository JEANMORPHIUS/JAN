### **5. Intent vs Impact**

Intent: What was meant, what was planned, what was intended. Impact: What actually happened, what was experienced, what was felt.

Good intent does not guarantee good impact. Systems designed with good intent can still cause harm. Impact matters more than intent. We must debug based on impact, not just intent.

When debugging systems, consider: What was the intent? What was the actual impact? How do we align intent with impact? How do we honor both?

---

### **6. Reactions vs Planned Actions**

Reactions: Spontaneous response to stimuli, immediate response, emotional response. Planned Actions: Intentional design, thoughtful response, aligned action.

Both have their place. Reactions are necessary for survival. Planned actions are necessary for transformation. The art is knowing when to use which.

When debugging systems, consider: Are we reacting or planning? Are we responding or creating? Are we in survival mode or transformation mode? How do we balance both?
