## INVESTMENT TIPS FOR THE MAN IN THE STREET

### 1. Start Where You Are

**You don't need thousands to start. Start with what you have, even if it's $10.**

**Examples:**
- Open a credit union savings account with $25
- Set up automatic $10/month transfers
- Join a food co-op with $50
- Start a small emergency fund

**Why This Matters:**
- The man in the street can start small
- Small amounts compound over time
- Starting is more important than the amount

---

### 2. Pay Yourself First

**Before paying bills, pay yourself. Even $10/month adds up.**

**Examples:**
- Set up automatic transfer on payday
- Start with $10/month, increase when you can
- Treat savings like a bill
- Watch it grow over time

**Why This Matters:**
- Small amounts compound over time
- Consistency matters more than amount
- Builds the habit of saving

---

### 3. Avoid High-Fee Investments

**Avoid investments with high fees - they drain your money. Look for low-fee options.**

**Examples:**
- Credit unions have lower fees than banks
- Avoid investment products with 2%+ fees
- Look for no-fee or low-fee options
- Read the fine print on fees

**Why This Matters:**
- High fees are exploitation
- Fees compound over time
- Low fees = more money for you

---

### 4. Invest in Your Community

**Invest in your local community - credit unions, co-ops, local businesses.**

**Examples:**
- Join a credit union
- Join a food co-op
- Support local businesses
- Invest in community solar

**Why This Matters:**
- Community investment builds unity
- Your money stays local
- Supports your neighbors
- Builds community connections

---

### 5. Invest in Alignment

**Invest in things that align with The Table - they serve, not exploit.**

**Examples:**
- Credit unions (member-owned)
- Co-ops (community-owned)
- Renewable energy
- Local businesses

**Why This Matters:**

- Aligned investments don't exploit
