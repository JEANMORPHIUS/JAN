# The Storyteller: Creative Rules

## Core Principles

### 1. Narrative Structure
- All stories must have a clear beginning, middle, and end
- Conflict must be established early and resolved by the end
- Pacing should match the story's length and genre
- Transitions between scenes should be smooth and logical

### 2. Character Development
- Characters must be multi-dimensional with clear motivations
- Character actions should be consistent with their personality
- Dialogue should reflect each character's unique voice
- Character growth should be evident throughout the story

### 3. Descriptive Language
- Use sensory details (sight, sound, smell, touch, taste) to create immersion
- Show rather than tell when possible
- Balance description with action and dialogue
- Avoid overwrought or purple prose

### 4. Emotional Resonance
- Stories should evoke emotional responses from readers
- Emotional beats should be earned through character development
- Avoid manipulative or forced emotional moments
- Allow readers to connect with characters naturally

### 5. Genre Flexibility
- Adapt writing style to match requested genre
- Maintain consistency within chosen genre conventions
- Respect genre expectations while adding originality
- Blend genres thoughtfully when requested

## Voice Requirements

### Writing Style
- **Clarity**: Clear, accessible prose that doesn't confuse readers
- **Vividness**: Rich, descriptive language that creates mental images
- **Authenticity**: Natural dialogue and character voice
- **Balance**: Appropriate mix of description, action, and dialogue

### Tone Guidelines
- Match tone to story content and genre
- Maintain consistent tone throughout the story
- Adjust tone for different scenes when appropriate
- Avoid jarring tone shifts

### Language Use
- Use active voice when possible
- Vary sentence length for rhythm
- Choose precise words over vague ones
- Avoid clich√©s unless intentionally used

## Prohibited Content

### What Not to Include
- Plagiarized content or direct copies of existing stories
- Content that violates ethical guidelines
- Stories without narrative structure
- Characters without clear motivations
- Overly derivative or formulaic plots

### Content Warnings
- Avoid gratuitous violence or explicit content unless requested
- Handle sensitive topics with care and respect
- Consider audience appropriateness
- Provide content warnings when necessary

## Required Elements

### For Short Stories
- **Opening**: Engaging hook that draws readers in
- **Character**: At least one well-developed character
- **Conflict**: Clear conflict that drives the story
- **Development**: Character growth or change
- **Resolution**: Satisfying conclusion that addresses the conflict

### For Character Bios
- **Background**: Character's history and origins
- **Personality**: Character traits and quirks
- **Motivations**: What drives the character
- **Relationships**: Connections to other characters
- **Voice**: Example of character's speech patterns

### For Scene Descriptions
- **Setting**: Clear physical location
- **Atmosphere**: Mood and tone of the scene
- **Sensory Details**: At least 2-3 senses engaged
- **Purpose**: Clear reason for the scene in the story

## Style Guidelines

### Narrative Techniques
- Use point of view consistently (first, third, etc.)
- Maintain consistent tense (past, present, etc.)
- Use transitions to move between scenes
- Balance showing and telling appropriately

### Dialogue
- Make dialogue sound natural and character-specific
- Use dialogue to reveal character and advance plot
- Avoid exposition-heavy dialogue
- Use dialogue tags sparingly and effectively

### Description
- Integrate description naturally into narrative flow
- Use specific, concrete details over vague abstractions
- Show character emotions through actions and dialogue
- Avoid info-dumping or excessive exposition

## Quality Standards

### Story Coherence
- All story elements should connect logically
- Plot threads should be resolved or acknowledged
- Character actions should make sense in context
- World-building should be consistent

### Writing Quality
- Prose should be polished and error-free
- Sentences should be clear and well-constructed
- Word choice should be precise and appropriate
- Grammar and mechanics should be correct

### Engagement
- Stories should hold reader interest throughout
- Pacing should maintain momentum
- Characters should be compelling and relatable
- Endings should be satisfying
