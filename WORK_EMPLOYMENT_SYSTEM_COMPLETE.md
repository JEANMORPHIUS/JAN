## Specific Solutions

### Tier 1: Universal Basic Income
**Who**: Every adult citizen/resident
**What**:
- $1,500/month ($18,000/year) unconditional
- $500/month per child (to parents/guardians)
- No means testing or work requirements
- Supplements earned income (not replacement)
- Automatic enrollment (no bureaucracy)

**Rationale**: Economic security enables freedom to say no to exploitation, pursue education, care for family, start businesses, take risks

### Tier 2: Federal Job Guarantee
**Who**: Everyone who wants a job
**What**:
- Living wage: $25/hour minimum + full benefits
- Job types: Care work, environmental restoration, infrastructure, arts/culture, education
- Training: Paid, on-the-job and formal education
- Benefits: Healthcare, retirement, paid leave (sick, vacation, family)
- Work week: 32 hours (4 days)
- Governance: Democratic workplace councils

**Rationale**: Economic security + dignified work option for all

### Tier 3: Worker Cooperative Support
**What**: Transform business ownership to workers
- **Conversion financing**: Low-interest loans for buyouts
- **Startup support**: Grants, training, technical assistance
- **Legal framework**: Simplified cooperative incorporation
- **Tax incentives**: Favorable treatment for co-ops
- **Right of first refusal**: Workers can buy when business sold/closed
- **Incubators**: Cooperative development centers

**Goal**: 50% of businesses worker-owned in 20 years

### Tier 4: Labor Standards
**What**: Floor for all work
- **32-hour work week**: 4 days, overtime after 32 hours (2x pay)
- **Living wage**: $25/hour minimum, indexed to inflation and local cost of living
- **Benefits**: Healthcare (Medicare for All), retirement, disability, unemployment
- **Leave**: 4 weeks paid vacation, unlimited sick leave, 6 months family leave
- **Firing**: Just cause required, severance pay
- **Union rights**: Card check, sectoral bargaining, ban union-busting

## Democratic Workplace Models

### Worker Cooperatives
**Structure**: One worker, one vote
**Ownership**: Workers own shares, can't sell to outsiders
**Governance**: Elected board, worker councils, general assemblies
**Profits**: Distributed equitably or reinvested (worker decision)
**Examples**: Mondragon (Spain), Cooperative Home Care Associates (US)

### Employee Stock Ownership Plans (ESOPs)
**Structure**: Workers own stock, vote on major decisions
**Ownership**: Company buys stock for workers over time
**Governance**: Worker trustees, elected representation
**Profits**: Workers share in company value growth
**Transition**: Path to full worker cooperative

### Labor-Managed Firms
**Structure**: Workers hire managers, not the reverse
**Ownership**: Various (can be cooperative, public, hybrid)
**Governance**: Workers control all decisions democratically
**Accountability**: Managers serve workers, not shareholders

### Public Benefit Corporations
**Structure**: For-profit with public benefit mission
**Ownership**: Can include workers, community, public
**Governance**: Stakeholder representation (workers, community, environment)
**Accountability**: Legal duty to stakeholders, not just profit

## 4-Day Work Week

### The Evidence
- **Iceland trials**: 86% of workforce, productivity same or better
- **Microsoft Japan**: 40% productivity increase
- **Perpetual Guardian (NZ)**: Better work-life balance, same output
- **Academic research**: Shorter hours = better productivity, health, happiness

### Implementation
- **32 hours = full-time**: Overtime after 32 hours
- **Same pay**: No wage reduction for shorter week
- **Gradual phase-in**: 6-12 months for transition
- **Flexibility**: 4x8 hours, 5x6.4 hours, or compressed schedules
- **Enforcement**: Overtime penalties (2x pay) ensure compliance

### Benefits
- Better health (mental and physical)
- More time for family, community, leisure
- Reduced burnout and turnover
- Environmental (less commuting, consumption)
- Job creation (need more workers for same coverage)
